What the student sees on the door
Your roommate in veterinary school failed her first anatomy test. She was very upset as this is the first failing grade she had ever received. She was determined to bring up her grade on the next exam so she studied every night after school from the time she got home until 1:00am including the weekends. The harder she studied, the more stressed out she became. Despite all of this hard work, she did poorly on the next exam as well. By the end of the first semester she is in danger of failing out. How do you advise her?
Probing Questions

  1. What do you think of her study habits?

  2. Do you think studying longer always means you learn more?

  3. If the student has not already acknowledged it, when do you think your roommate should seek professional help?

  4. What are some avenues they might pursue for professional help?

Rater Sheet
This is a common scenario for students with academic difficulty. These students often lack appropriate study skills and coping skills. Follow-up information may include The roommate does not sleep very well or eat well and does not get any exercise.
