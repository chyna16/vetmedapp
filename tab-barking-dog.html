What the student sees on the door
A young family is considering having their 2 year-old FS Beagle be debarked. It is not illegal in NY but many people believe debarking is unethical because it is a surgery that has no benefit to the dog. However, the owners live in a rent-controlled apartment building in Manhattan and the building manager is threatening eviction based on other tenant complaints. They do not want to give up the dog, the children are very attached. What do you think about this situation? Please be advised that there is no right or wrong answer.
Brief Description Of Procedure
Jan 1, 2019 JAVMA news reaffirmed the existing policy on canine devocalization. There is broad opposition to the procedure however exceptions are recognized. The current policy reads as follows. “Canine devocalization should on be performed by qualified, licensed veterinarians as a final alternative to euthanasia after behavioral modification to correct excessive vocalization has failed and after potential complications have been discussed with the owner”
Problem Questions
  1. Do you think there are any other solutions besides surgery that the owners might consider? (Behavioral modification, training)
  2. Are there any conditions in which you could justify the procedure?
  3. Do you feel the same way about other controversial procedures such as ear cropping, declawing cats, tail docking?
  4. What about castrating male dogs and doing a complete OHE on female dogs? There are some that believe these procedures are often unnecessary and if done too early cause bone growth abnormalities and predispose to some cancers.
